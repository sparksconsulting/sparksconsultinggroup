The Hidden Revenue in Your Spreadsheets

January 10, 2026 By Dyllan Sparks

Your spreadsheets contain clues about revenue opportunities you're currently missing. Underpriced products. Underutilized customers. Inefficient operations. These insights are buried in your data, waiting to be discovered.

Where the Money Hides

Revenue opportunities hide in several places: pricing analysis (products selling below margin targets), customer analysis (high-value customers being under-served), product mix analysis (high-margin products being de-emphasized), and operational efficiency (unnecessary costs in fulfillment or support).

A Real Example

One client discovered that 30% of their "best-selling" product line actually operated at breakeven or below. They were pouring effort into products that didn't generate profit. By reallocating that effort, they increased overall margins by 18% without raising prices or cutting service.

How to Start Looking

Analyze Your Pricing

Compare what you're charging against your actual costs. Are some customers getting significantly better prices than others for the same service? That's often an untapped pricing opportunity.

Segment Your Revenue

Break down revenue by product, customer, channel, and region. You'll likely find that 80% of your profit comes from 20% of your offerings or customers. That's where to focus.

Look for Inefficiencies

Track operational costs by product or customer. Some customers might require disproportionate support. Some products might have high fulfillment costs. These are opportunities for optimization or repricing.

The data you need is likely already in your spreadsheets. Let's help you find it.
